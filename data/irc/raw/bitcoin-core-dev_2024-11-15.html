16:28 <sipa> jamesob15: there are two models of looking at the ledger that are common. the "wallet" model, where addresses are entry points into a wallet, but don't hold a balance of their own; it's the wallet that holds a balance in aggregate by having control of a number of coins. You can't "spend from" an address at all, because that is not where coins are held.
16:29 <sipa> the other is the "explorer" model, where addresses hold a balance of their own, and transactions have to-address and from-addresses, which increment and decrement that balance
16:29 <sipa> both of these models are wrong (addresses don't exist at all at the protocol level), but some models are useful
16:29 <sipa> i'm strongly of the opinion that the "explorer" model is harmful
16:31 <sipa> here, not too long ago: https://www.reddit.com/r/Bitcoin/comments/1fkjx2o/comment/lnwgnzp/

16:31 <jamesob15> Asking the wallet to "spend from this address" is a convenient interface in that case.
