19:20 <jeremyrubin> The idea is that you'd make a "rolling coin join" where to start it's N inputs 1 output that is N-N or split in half (via CTV) and recurse (n/2-n/2, n/2-n/2) where leaf nodes are single keys.
19:21 <jeremyrubin> You can also, like a normal coin pool, add funds (add input -> N+1-N+1 multisig) or remove funds (N-1 of N-1)
19:22 <jeremyrubin> You can also make payments out of the coin pool directly (e.g, make payment decreasing Bob's balance in the coin pool but not publicly seen) to external (or internal) recipient
19:23 <jeremyrubin> If you don't *want* the rolling style coinjoin then you can ignore the payment pool and directly expand the tree via congestion control. You can mess with parameters, but this ends up being able to be a bit more efficient for locking in the root and expanding via a 2nd txn, especially if your outputs have different 'priority' for being spent (bits
