00:08 < gmaxwell> so at devcore one of the things I talked about was some analysis taken from monitoring miners and mining pools. Someone collected data from all accessible stratum endpoints over several months
00:10 < gmaxwell> and from it I can extract data like how much time from the first pool working on extending block X was it until the 2nd, 3rd, ... nth pool. Taking the time after the first pool to reach half the pools, fit a linear model reasonably well.

00:12 < gmaxwell> 20:40 <gmaxwell> (Intercept) 2.491e+00 2.674e-01 9.318 <2e-16 ***
00:12 < gmaxwell> 20:40 <gmaxwell> size 1.106e-05 4.025e-07 27.474 <2e-16 ***
00:13 < gmaxwell> which means a 2.491 second constant delay, plus 732kbit/sec. (1/1.106*10^-5*8)
00:15 < gmaxwell> interestingly, plugging this into an orphaning calculation vs the subsidy---- suggests that the final byte of the block should have a feerate of ((((e^(-1/600*(2.491+((1000000)/90415.91)))))*25)-(((e^(-1/600*(2.491+((1000001)/90415.91)))))*25))*1000 = .00045054 BTC per 1000 bytes, or otherwise you're losing money just considering the subsidy.
00:15 < gmaxwell> Though reality is not that simple, because of hashpower distribution dynamics, large miners don't really care if it takes small miners a long time to get their blocks.
00:17 < gmaxwell> so, take with a metric ton of salt, but I thought it was interesting that these figures are in roughly this magnitude.
