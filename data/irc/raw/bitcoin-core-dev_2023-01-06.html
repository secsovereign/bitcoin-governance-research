20:23 <jamesob> Probably dumb question, but why does `uint256::ToString()` (-> `HexStr()`) print out the blob in reversed byte order?
20:26 brunoerg has quit [Ping timeout: 252 seconds]
20:27 sipsorcery has joined #bitcoin-core-dev
20:29 <sipa> Because that's what it has always done, really.
20:29 <sipa> And people expect txids and block hashes in hex notation to be reserved.
20:30 <sipa> Historically, that was because txids/block hashes were 256-bit numbers constructed by interpreting the double-sha256 output as a little-endian integer, and then printing it out for human consumption in hex (where humans expect numbers in big endian).
20:31 <sipa> But now uint256 is just a byte array wrapper, the behavior is maintained because block hashes and txids are always printed in reverse.
20:32 brunoerg has joined #bitcoin-core-dev
20:32 <sipa> (block hashes are still interpreted a little-endian numbers for the purpose of comparing PoW to the target, but that's handled by the arith_uint256 class now)
